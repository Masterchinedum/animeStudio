## 1. Adaptation premise

In Vesper City, status is not a reputation: it is a weapon. The Ledger converts
secrets, debts, political favours, and criminal proof into an Executive Tier that
controls who can own property, receive protection, or survive public exposure.

Tier-4 forensic accountant **Ren Sato** is ordered by the Tier-8 chairman **Kang
Dae-Jung** to investigate a wave of executive collapses. The dead all appear to
have used their own biometrics to surrender their collateral. Ren discovers the
attacks carry the signature of his private escape code, the Erasure Protocol.

The investigation binds Ren to **Mei-Ling Vance**, Kang's brilliant and isolated
wife. She may be implicated in the crimes, framed by stolen biometrics, or using Ren
for an escape plan of her own. Their attraction stays restrained and consequential:
it is never explicit, and every moment of intimacy changes the balance of power.

The season's first five episodes are a contained noir investigation. By the end,
Ren and Mei-Ling learn Kang is not merely containing a leak—he is dismantling his own
board to seize the master control of the Vesper Blue offshore reserve.

## 2. Non-negotiable series rules

- **The Ledger is grounded near-future infrastructure, not magic.** It operates
  through financial access, biometrics, surveillance, contracts, and enforcement.
- **Every Tier change must have a visible practical consequence:** assets freeze,
  doors deny entry, glass walls become opaque, security protection disappears, or
  someone becomes legally expendable.
- **The Sacrifice Metric is the moral engine.** Advancement always creates a debt,
  betrays a person, or exposes a secret; nobody levels up through combat power.
- **Ren wins by observation, preparation, and compromise—not invincibility.**
- **Violence is short, frightening, and consequential.** The show is a suspense
  noir, not an action spectacle.
- **Adult material is non-explicit.** Use eye contact, interrupted touches, silence,
  clothing, physical distance, and aftermath. Do not depict sexual activity.
- **No legacy names or story facts are permitted** in new episode, character, scene,
  shot, or image prompts: Elias Venn, Mara Vale, or the former Tier-9 boss premise
  do not belong to this version.

## 4. Visual language

### Overall look

Grounded, cinematic adult anime: rain-polished glass, concrete, brushed steel,
failing municipal infrastructure, and carefully rationed neon. Vesper City should
feel wealthy at height and predatory at street level. Avoid generic cyberpunk clutter,
magic effects, exaggerated super-deformed reactions, or constant holograms.

### Colour and camera rules

- **Kang Global:** cold blue, black, silver, restrained amber; locked-off frames and
  symmetric compositions that make people look evaluated.
- **Vance Haute Couture / Mei-Ling:** slate, emerald, ivory, violet server light;
  elegant framing that becomes increasingly claustrophobic.
- **Slag and Blue Districts:** sodium orange, wet cobalt, green spill, practical
  smoke; handheld or gently unstable framing only when Ren loses control.
- **Ledger events:** do not use spell-like effects. Use interface typography,
  biometric readouts, changing access lights, frosted glass, silence, and the
  reaction of people who understand what a demotion means.
- **Intimacy:** close framing on hands, breath, interrupted glances, and a choice to
  stay. Cut away before explicit contact.

### Recurring visual motifs

- Rain falling across windows like a distorted audit trail.
- Doors, elevators, and glass that stop admitting people after their rank changes.
- Analog objects as refuges: Ren's brass clock, physical tokens, paper bonds,
  hardware keys.
- Reflections that place Ren and Mei-Ling in the same frame before they trust one
  another.

## 5. Season 1, Block A: five-episode plan

### Episode 1 — **The Red Audit**

**Adapted chapters:** 1–3  
**Episode question:** Who is using Ren's code, and why is Kang suddenly afraid?

Cold open on the aftermath of Director Han's fall, then cut to Kang commissioning
Ren's audit. Ren receives temporary high-level access while Marcus is assigned to
watch him. At Mei-Ling's charity gala, she demonstrates that she knows Ren's private
movements and plants a micro-drive on him. Ren and Yuki decrypt it, uncovering the
blackmailer known as **the Tailor** and a list of future targets; the decryption
triggers a security sweep.

**Episode turn:** Ren destroys his secondary server and discovers a fragment of his
own Erasure Protocol in Han's records.

**Priority set pieces:** Kang's penthouse office; the Vance gala showroom; Ren's
air-gapped audit room; the first silent security sweep.

### Episode 2 — **The Second Fall**

**Adapted chapters:** 4–6  
**Episode question:** Can Ren protect Kenji while the next target is publicly erased?

Ren visits Kenji in the Slag District and spends half of his escape fund relocating
him after corporate sweepers arrive. Kenji reveals a scavenged comm-device receiving
Tier-8 pings. Ren then watches Vice-President Choi collapse from Tier 5 to Tier 1
during an interview. Choi is taken to Marcus's interrogation room; Ren finds the
blackmail payments passing through Vance Haute Couture, and Choi is poisoned before
he can explain the arrangement.

**Episode turn:** Ren now has proof that Mei-Ling's company is involved—and the
knowledge that someone can murder inside Kang's secured holding cells.

**Priority set pieces:** Slag workshop; a Tier-1 office transformation; the
interrogation room; Choi's poisoned water glass.

### Episode 3 — **Silk and Steel**

**Adapted chapters:** 7–9  
**Episode question:** Is Mei-Ling a culprit, a victim, or Ren's most dangerous ally?

Ren confronts Mei-Ling at her studio. She counters his evidence with a threat to
expose the Erasure Protocol and offers help moving Kenji if Ren protects Inspector
Jiro. Ren meets Sophia Thorne, who rejects money and trades a three-minute masking
token for an audit favour. Sophia reveals that the physical leak terminal is inside
Kang's residence. At a private estate dinner, Mei-Ling signals the terminal's
coordinates while Kang places Marcus directly over Ren's audit.

**Episode turn:** Ren loses operational independence just as he learns where the
leaks originate.

**Priority set pieces:** Mei-Ling's fashion studio; Sophia's secure tea house;
Kang's ridge estate dinner.

### Episode 4 — **The Signature**

**Adapted chapters:** 10–12  
**Episode question:** Whose hand is actually operating Kang's terminal?

During the estate dinner, Mei-Ling distracts Marcus while Ren copies the terminal
logs. The logs identify Mei-Ling's biometrics as the authorisation for the leaks.
When Ren confronts her, she reveals that Kang's clinic has sedated her and harvested
her iris and dermal prints to build a scapegoat. Yuki then finds a hidden stream from
the estate terminal to Apex Infrastructure and admits that Apex is pressuring her
with her own secret collateral.

**Episode turn:** Ren and Mei-Ling choose a real alliance; Ren will redirect Kang's
paranoia toward Apex while secretly pursuing Kang's biometric key.

**Priority set pieces:** Kang's private study; Mei-Ling's evidence of coercion;
Yuki's packet-routing discovery.

### Episode 5 — **The Glass Penthouse**

**Adapted chapters:** 13–15  
**Episode question:** What is Kang truly taking from his own people?

Inspector Jiro threatens Kenji's safety unless Ren trades Han's audit. Marcus then
seizes the audit department, freezes Yuki's terminal, and forces Ren to hide the
Erasure Protocol in a brass clock. Ren reaches Mei-Ling's penthouse through a forged
access route. They work all night on her hidden servers, share a restrained moment of
trust, and identify the pattern behind the victims: all were Vesper Blue key holders.

**Episode turn / block finale:** Kang has staged the collapses to revoke the board's
biometric authorisations and consolidate the Vesper Blue reserve beneath his own
master key. Ren and Mei-Ling are no longer simply investigating the crime; they are
inside its mechanism.

**Priority set pieces:** Blue District alley; counter-audit seizure; Mei-Ling's
server room and rain-streaked penthouse.

## 6. Episode 1 production guardrails

Episode 1 is the only episode that should enter visual production next.

- Target **10–14 story beats** and approximately **35–50 final shots**. This is a
  planning target, not an instruction to generate all images at once.
- Lock Ren, Mei-Ling, Kang, Marcus, and Yuki reference portraits before rendering
  any Episode 1 keyframe.
- Generate one approved keyframe and one short motion proof for each major visual
  environment before scaling the shot batch.
- Keep the climax on Ren recognising the Erasure Protocol signature. The ending
  should be a close, quiet shock—not an action sequence.
- Use two to four-second clips only for a single motivated action or reaction. Edit
  through cause-and-effect; do not concatenate unrelated model clips.
